The future of gardening is knocking at the worlds door: HECTOR is born.

The Hedge Cutting, Trimming and Observing Robot. Hedge Cutting never was this entertaining.
Autonomous, efficient and safe.

Autonomy

HECTOR operates completely independently, requiring no human intervention during hedge cutting. Advanced AI navigation ensures precise trimming while avoiding obstacles and maintaining perfect hedge geometry throughout your garden.

Precision

State-of-the-art sensors and cutting mechanisms deliver millimeter-precise hedge shaping. Our patented blade system adapts to different hedge types and densities, ensuring optimal results every time with minimal waste.

Conservation

Zero emissions operation with whisper-quiet performance protects your environment and neighborhood peace. Solar charging capabilities and biodegradable cutting oil make HECTOR the sustainable choice for modern gardening.

Our Mission

Every hedge in this world won’t have to be cut by human power anymore. Hedges will be the accelerator to battle climate change. With HECTOR this will be not just a dream but reality.

Facts and Opportunities:
8.900.000 km of hedges in
Europe
There are over 8.900.000 km of hedges in Europe. Those are the habitat of a multitude of different species of animals especially birds. Hedges are the sign of prosperity and luxury, pathing every property of renomated castles, golf clubs, etc.
120.000.000 hours each year spent on
hedge cutting
Up to this day there are over 120.000.000 hours spent on hedge cutting every year. This accounts for a lot of manual labor which is to this day done by hand. With the ever so fast increasing “Fachkräftemangel” and demographic change there wont be enough manpower to do this work.
3.5 tons of CO2 conversed into O2
per km of hedge
Hedges are one of the biggest catalysators of conversion of Co2 to 02. They are key components in the fight against climate change and through different European initiatives are set to be chosen as the hope of the fight for a clean climate without the cost of optical downsides.

Our Story

2020
2020
Birth of the idea of HECTOR:
With the start of Covid. Our founders were sitting ain their home on a sunny summer day. They had to cut the hedges of their home garden where tehere they got the crazy idea to just build a hedge cutting robot with some wood bought at the next utilities store.
2021
Initial Concepts and Key Acquisitions
In 2021, the founders laid the groundwork for their robotic platform by creating the first sketches, technical plans, and concept designs. During this phase, they also made their initial key acquisitions, securing essential equipment and resources that would enable the development and prototyping of their innovative solution.
2022
Proof of Concept
Development of the first proof of concept. A small version of HECTOR which should have all the core functions. Founders started to place strong emphasis on expanding their expertise in the areas most critical to the company's success. Each founder invested in targeted learning to complement their personal strengths and to ensure that the team as a whole could cover all aspects of product development and growth.
2023
First Prototype Completed
In 2023, the founders successfully completed the very first prototype of their robotic platform. This milestone marked the transition from vision to reality, as their initial ideas and concepts were translated into a tangible product. By combining their diverse expertise in engineering, design, and business, the team was able to bring the prototype to life and create a solid foundation for future testing, refinement, and development.
2024
First Full Prototype and Initial External Contacts
In 2024, the founders completed the first fully functional prototype of their robotic platform, marking a major step forward in its development. During the same year, they established their first contacts with governmental funding programs, patent offices, and potential investors. In addition, they reached out to their first major prospective customers and successfully received Letters of Intent (LOIs) from institutions such as Schloss Versailles and the City of Tübingen, laying the groundwork for future pilot projects and collaborations.
2025
EXIST Funding and MVP Development
In 2025, the founders successfully secured their first major public funding through the EXIST program, providing a significant boost to their project. With this support, they began developing the first market-ready MVP of their robotic platform, designed to be fully functional and suitable for initial pilot projects. This stage allowed the team to test their solutions in real-world scenarios, gather critical feedback, and refine the platform for future growth and commercial application.
